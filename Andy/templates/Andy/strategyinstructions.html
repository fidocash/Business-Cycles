Point 1):

You will provide your best guess about what wage the employer will offer in this round.
You can increase your final earnings according to your accuracy.

Accuracy of Guesses: We will pay you based on the accuracy of your guess about this wage offer. If your guess matches the wage actually offered you will earn $5. Otherwise, you will earn nothing.


You will earn money as follows:
  • the computer will randomly select one of your guesses.
  • If the guess is within 0.1 of the actual amount of effort, then you will earn $5. Otherwise, you will earn nothing.

Suppose for example that:
  • the employer offers a wage equal to $4
  • if you guess that the offered wage will be $4, then you earn $5
  • If you guess that the wage equals any other possible number, then you earn nothing

Point 2):

We will also ask you how much effort you are willing to provide for each possible wage that the employer can offer.

Your earnings for this task are determined as follows: for whatever wage the employer actually offers, we will use the effort you agreed to provide at that wage level (described in point 1 above) as your actual effort response.

For example, suppose you have agreed to provide some level of effort for each possible wage. Now, suppose that the employer agrees to offer a wage of $6 and that, during the effort response task described above, you agreed to provide effort 1.5 for a wage offer of $6. Then your earnings for this task are determined in the same way they are for a normal period where the employer offers a wage of $6 and you provide effort of 1.5 in response.

Payoff payment in this period:

  • Your payoff in this period will be determined either by the accuracy of your guess about what effort the worker will provide (Point 1) or the wage the employer offers (Point 2).
  • The source of this additional payoff (Point 1 or Point 2) will be randomly chosen and communicated at the end of the experiment.
  • However, you will be able to see the result of Point 2) upon the end of this period.
